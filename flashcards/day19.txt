\[\text{What is a Partitioned Matrix?}\]	\[\text{A matrix that is divided into blocks of other matrices (for multiplication you have to keep suitable dimensions)}\]\[A=\begin{bmatrix}A_{11}&A_{12}\\A_{21}&A_{22}\end{bmatrix}\]
\[\text{How to find an inverse of}\;A=\begin{bmatrix}A_{11}&A_{12}\\0&A_{22}\end{bmatrix}\quad\quad(A_{11}:p\times p,\quad A_{22}:q\times q)\]	\[\begin{bmatrix}A_{11}&A_{12}\\0&A_{22}\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}B_{11}&B_{12}\\B_{21}&A_{22}\end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix}I_p&0\\0&I_q\end{bmatrix}\]\[\text{Solve for all the matrices B after multiplication and get: }\begin{bmatrix}A_{11}&A_{12}\\0&A_{22}\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}A^{-1}_{11}&-A^{-1}_{11}A_{12}A^{-1}_{22}\\0&A^{-1}_{22}\end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix}I_p&0\\0&I_q\end{bmatrix}\]
\[\text{What is LU factorization and how to use it?}\]	\[\text{It is a method of dividing a matrix into 2 parts: L (lower triangular matrix) and U (echelon form of A) to solve}\;A\vec{x}=\vec{b}\]\[A\vec{x}=\vec{b}=L(U\vec{x})=\vec{b}\quad\Longrightarrow\quad U\vec{x}=\vec{y}\text{,    solve for y:}\;\;L\vec{y}=\vec{b}\]
\[\text{What is an algorithm for LU Factorization?}\]	\[(E_p\cdots E_2E_1)A=U\implies A=(E_p\cdots E_2E_1)^{-1} U=LU\implies L=(E_p\cdots E_2E_1)^{-1}I\]\[\text{It means we can either record all elementary matrices during row reduction}\]\[\text{Or just record column by column how many rows we reduced from each (if we reduced -2 1st rows, then put -2 in that cell)}\]
\[\text{What you cannot do in LU factorization? (2)}\]	\[\text{1. Interchange rows}\]\[\text{2. Scale rows (e.g. 0 5 to 0 1)}\]
